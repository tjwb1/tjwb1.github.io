Web Development Project Report

Toby Box

Introduction

I decided to create a topical magazine website that focusses on footwear, watches and men's style. I chose this as my subject as I wanted to work on something that interests me and allows me to really try and create a connection with visitors to the website. The aim was to take visitors on a journey from footwear, through watches, to complete outfits across the five pages, so it followed a logical sequence and each page built on the previous. For each page I aimed to use something slightly different as a main feature, but I wanted the style to be consistent across the site and the theme and colour scheme to remain constant. I was very keen that the style of the page match the subject and conveyed an upmarket and classically stylish image. When considering the structure for the website I wanted to try and make the website flow easily from one page to the next, so I decided to use five individual pages and not have sub-pages. I feel that a linear structure aids navigation and keeps the format simple, allowing users to navigate through the site easily and in a logical order. As the website is limited to five pages, I felt that a hierarchical structure would overly complicate the site and would inhibit the flow and the navigation journey I wanted to promote.

Inspiration

A few years ago, I made a big career decision and to celebrate and mark the occasion I bought a nice watch and some nice shoes. Since then I have become more and more interested in men's style, with a particular focus on watches and footwear. When the final project topic was released, I saw it as the perfect opportunity to work on a subject I enjoyed as a topical magazine website. I follow a number of YouTube channels, Mr Porter, He Spoke Style, You're Terrific and Watch Collecting Strategy to name a few. Each of those channels has a unique style and I was inspired to create my website to be cool, stylish and in keeping with the theme and subject. I browsed websites for inspiration and Sneakers-Magazine.com was perhaps my biggest inspiration for layout. I liked the clean look of the pictures and how the picture tiles had no border and the text was placed outside the image. I decided to replicate that on my home page but change the colour scheme. The layout of Mr Porters journal section and the Permanent Style website were a real inspiration for how to format my articles and images and I felt that the fonts and colours reflected a very 'grown up' subject. I didn't copy the colour schemes, but I liked how the contrast of text and articles worked so I tried to implement a similar contrast on my website. I also watched a number of interesting videos on YouTube about website design but one that stuck out to me was "1 design trend in 2022 that I think will be BIG! (7 examples)" by DesignCourse. I didn't copy any one thing from the video, but it gave me inspiration to try new things such as scroll effects, picture enlargement and filter menus. Those three areas of inspiration really formed the visual design of my website and helped me create an individual style that I felt matched the subject matter.

Accessibility

I have taken a number of steps to ensure accessibility in my website. The 3 main ways my site is accessible are:
  1. I have used clear alt text for images to ensure screen readers can understand exactly what the image is portraying.
  2. I have ensured that I use relative text sizes and accounted for resizing text when changing screen sizes. This enables the text to scale depending on the other content of the page and the page size. I have also ensured that when a user zooms in to the page the text is still readable.
  3. I have ensured that the site is keyboard friendly and users are able to 'tab' around the pages with ease. I have achieved this by using focus in some areas and ensuring that articles are linked by 'a href' elements to aid navigation.

Usability

When creating my website, usability was extremely important to me and I aimed to achieve it across three main areas, Navigation, Content Clarity and Responsiveness. When looking at navigation I decided a simple, linear structure for the website would ensure all the content was easily available in a clear, logical order for the website users. I made the navigation bar common to all pages and made sure that the menu headings reacted when scrolled over, by using the hover property in CSS. Visually seeing a change in colour and underline means that it is very easy to see which menu item you are selecting. This was applied to both the navigation bar and the footer menu to ensure parity and consistency. When creating my content I wanted to ensure that not only the style reflected the subject but that the content was clear and easy to see and read. I picked fonts and colours that I knew stood out and created contrast to enhance viewing. I picked images that were in keeping with the style of the website but also were clear, well photographed and unambiguous. I kept text outside of articles to a minimum to aid in scanning the website and made sure that the layout drew the users eyes to the main content. While looking at responsiveness my goal was to add to the accessibility of the site while making sure that it still maintained it's style and image when looked at on different screen sizes. One area that I focussed heavily on was the navigation menu and ensuring that at the appropriate screen size it turned to a hamburger menu. This achieved two things, the menu was easy to use as I included the exact same heading and animations highlighting the selected item, and, hamburger menus are familiar as they are widely used so I added that comfort and familiarity to the website.

Learning

  1. Flex box Use
    Although the lectures briefly touched on Flexbox as a tool for laying out webpages, I found that I really needed to learn more myself to effectively use it in my website. I undertook the Udemy Web Development course concurrently to the lectures and it really helped me understand the specifics of Flexbox. I was able to then plan and construct my pages with a much better understanding of how the flex display property worked and how parent and child elements reacted based on their Flexbox settings. I felt that I used grid display in appropriate areas but really harnessed the power of Flexbox to create the majority of my website. I practiced often by creating simple pages with coloured boxes to learn how the different properties affected the layout and then applied what I had seen to my own website. A great example of this was when creating the Watches page, I was having some difficulty with how the articles were positioning themselves so created a simple, coloured boxes webpage and practiced using different Flexbox settings to get the right layout. I then transferred the settings to my page.
  2. Hamburger menu creation
    I knew at a very early stage that I wanted a hamburger menu in my mobile version of the site and I think it is familiar, current and stylish when using a mobile device or smaller screen. I found that a hamburger menu was a particularly difficult object to create with little experience in Web Design. I tried multiple YouTube tutorials for information and guidance on how to create the menu and settled on the framework from a YouTube video by Kevin Powell titled "Create a responsive navigation nav with no JS!". I then adapted the basics of the layout and method to suit my theme, colour scheme and menu items. I found it very difficult to work out how to size and place my menu in the exact position I wanted and not affect its action when in responsive mode or using smaller screens. I tried many different settings and this also helped me explore more CSS properties and gain a greater understanding of how everything fit and worked together.
  3. Picture resize functionality
    I wanted to have the ability to click on a picture to enlarge it and realised early on the Javascript was the easiest way to achieve this functionality. I managed to work out, by myself, how to apply it to the main article image on my home page using DOM functions and buttons. I tried a number of different CSS properties to make sure the image had the correct presentation and position. I couldn't work out how to apply my enlargeImg() function to multiple images so decided to try a different system for the Shoes and Boots pages. I asked a question on StackOverflow about my enlargeImg() function and was signposted to a previous topic and code solution. I used this code snippet as the basis of my page and then inserted it into the main section of my webpages. This allowed me to have the same header and footer but have a new structure within the main element of the body. This did mean I had to work through a number of problems caused by inherited properties which needed to be accounted for in the new code for it to work. I learned a lot about how to use existing code and refactor it to suit my ideas. The new structure for the Shoes and Boots pages now focussed on a series of expandable boxes which allowed me to use the Javascript function to enlarge the pictures across all the images. It was a very interesting problem to work through and I learned about containers and expandable elements and how to use event listeners to control them. This was perhaps the most difficult problem I worked through but also the most interesting.

Evaluation I

I think that the style, colours and theme of the website work well. I feel that they give a feeling of sophistication in keeping with a stylish menswear site. My menu system works well, and I think that the way it switches from a nav bar to a hamburger menu is well coded and responds well at various screen sizes. I am happy with the different methods of enlarging pictures that I have used; I feel that they bring some extra depth and complexity to the website. I am pleased with my Javascript functionality, I think the combination of self-written and refactored code adds some great effects to the website and enhances the user experience. I am pleased that I had already completed both Introduction to Programming modules prior to this course as the background Javascript knowledge really helped me understand some of the more difficult concepts. Along with the Javascript, I feel that the use of flex and grid layouts is appropriate and at the right level to enhance the website. It's a fairly simple design and I feel that my use of different layout systems ensures that the responsiveness and usability of the site is at the required standard.

Evaluation II

I think the majority of areas I feel that could be improved are as a result of the structure of the website. For example, I found sizing the embedded YouTube videos difficult and I feel that I could investigate better ways to implement those. While creating the website, as I decided on adding extra usability, I found that in order to either apply a new effect, or to use refactored code, I needed to change the structure and add new elements. This caused me many problems with inherited properties, and I spent a lot of time manipulating existing code to ensure the new effects worked and did not change the layout. I want to learn more about how properties are inherited and also about how functions bubble up in Javascript and I will become more familiar with the developer tools at my disposal to help with that. In the future, now I have learned a great deal about the design and how to implement various effects, I would structure my webpages differently and be more meticulous in my planning. I used Flexbox as the main display layout for my website as it seemed easier to work with and more powerful, but, looking at the structure of my website I feel that I could've made more use of grid and it would've perhaps been easier to work with. I decided against using bootstrap as I didn't feel it would give me any enhanced functionality but in the future I would definitely look at how to incorporate other frameworks.
